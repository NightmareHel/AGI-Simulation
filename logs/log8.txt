Day 1: Arrival and Exploration of Asakusa

Day 2: Meiji Shrine and Harajuku
Visit the Meiji Shrine, dedicated to the deified spirits of Emperor Meiji and his wife, Empress Shoken, which is free to visit. Take a walk down the trendy Harajuku street, known for its unique fashion styles, and explore the local shops and cafes. In the afternoon, experience a traditional Japanese tea ceremony, which can cost around 500-1,000 yen ($4.50-9 USD) per person. For dinner, try some delicious tsukemen at Tsukemen Gyoza no Ohsho in the Asakusa ward, with prices starting at around 800 yen ($7.25 USD) per person.

Day 3: Tokyo Skytree and Shibuya
Visit the Tokyo Skytree for panoramic views of the city, with entrance fees ranging from $15 to $30 per person. Combine this with a visit to the nearby Solamachi shopping and dining complex, which offers a wide range of affordable food options, including restaurants and cafes. In the evening, head to the Shibuya Crossing, one of the busiest intersections in the world, to experience the local nightlife. Explore the surrounding Shibuya area, which offers a wide range of shopping, dining, and entertainment options, including the famous Shibuya 109 department store.
